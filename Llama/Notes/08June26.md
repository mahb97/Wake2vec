### Three findings in one eval

1. **Geometric null, confirmed at 10%.** L_morph reads 0.0006 across all four batches at step 100, identical to step 0 and step 50. λ=50 produces zero observable movement in 100 steps. TinyLlama P3 took 1000 steps to establish this. Llama 1B P3 took 1000. 3B P3 strong settled it in 100. The fastest null in the lineup.

2. **L_device is noise.** Range 0.19 to 0.21 across batches, no monotonic trend, no triplet convergence forming. Identical behaviour to Llama 1B P3's random walk (0.185 to 0.210). The device-clustering objective is structurally unlearnable, not just on TinyLlama (32K vocab) and not just on Llama 1B (128K), but now also on Llama 3B (128K). Three architectures, same null.

3. **NEW: LM degradation under strong lambdas.** Val 5.61 vs P2's wall at 5.33 is a +0.28 increase. P3 strong is not just failing to move the auxiliary metrics, it is *actively pulling LoRA weights away from the LM-optimal P2 configuration*. The auxiliary objectives drag the model off its P2 minimum without producing the geometric structure they're asking for. The cost shows up as LM regression.~~ **(superseded by step 200 eval, see revision)**

### current arg: 

**Step 150 mid-cycle [LOSS] printouts:** per-batch LM mean dropped from ~5.17 at step 100 to ~4.75 at step 150. First substantial LM movement of the run, and it's downward. L_morph still pinned at 0.0006 across all four batches, L_device still noise (0.19-0.21). The LM descent isn't being driven by the auxiliary losses suddenly finding signal. It's the LM objective reorganising under aux constraints and finding a new minimum.

(revised at step 200): *strong auxiliary geometric pressure (λ_morph=50, λ_device=2) on 128K-vocab Llama at 3B parameters produces brief initial LM disruption followed by re-equilibration. The auxiliary objectives have no lasting effect on the geometric metrics they target OR on LM fit, beyond the first ~100 steps of recalibration. The model finds equilibria that satisfy LM and ignore the aux losses.*

1B P3 showed indifference while the 3B P3 shows the landscape pushing back.

### Timing and early-stop projection

EARLY_STOP_PATIENCE=5 at 100-step eval cadence means termination after 5 evals without val improvement. realistic end point is step 500 to 700.

**Step 200 eval confirms the transient reading:**

| Step | Train | Val | vs P2 wall |
|------|-------|-----|------------|
| 100 | 22.03 | 5.61 | +0.28 |
| 200 | 20.93 | **5.49** | **+0.16** |

Val dropped 0.12 in 100 steps. New best val of the run.

The "active LM degradation" reading was wrong. The +0.28 at step 100 was disequilibrium during the first 100 steps of aux pressure, not a persistent cost. The model is rebuilding LM fit while ignoring the aux losses. Strong λs cause brief LM disruption that the model recovers from.

The revised third finding is actually a **cleaner null** than the original reading: strong auxiliary pressure produces *no lasting effect* on anything. Not on the geometric metrics it targets. Not on LM after the first 100 steps of recalibration. Not on the embedding structure. The model treats the aux losses as noise it learns to route around. Pure landscape inertia.

## Notes

Phi-3 Mini launches as soon as 3B P3 terminates.

1. **Phi extends the 58% Wake-vocab-share cluster.** currently TinyLlama (58%) and Mistral (58%) sit there alone but Phi's 32K vocab puts it in the same cluster. third datapoint makes the smaller-model paradox a pattern across three architectures (TinyLlama base, Mistral SWA, Phi dense), not a TinyLlama/Mistral coincidence.

2. **"Textbook quality training data" is a new hypothesis.** Phi-3 was trained on filtered high-signal data designed for clean reasoning but the Wake is the corpus at maximum distance from that filter. does a model trained for cleanliness resist embedding injection that asks it to internalise chaos? if Phi generates Wake-style prose as densely as TinyLlama did, evidence the embedding injection overrides pretraining bias. if it doesn't, evidence pretraining priors gate what injection can do. 

Gemma 2 9B launches second. it extends architectural coverage (Google RMSNorm placement, gated activations) and confirms the high-vocab-low-injection pattern Qwen already shows.
