### New finding: widening train-val gap as the lasting effect

Comparison of train-val gap across the project:

| Regime | Train LM | Eval val | Gap |
|--------|----------|----------|-----|
| P2 normal (3B step 600) | ~5.24 | 5.33 | 0.09 |
| P3 strong (step 100) | ~5.17 | 5.61 | 0.44 |
| P3 strong (step 200) | ~4.75 | 5.49 | 0.74 |

The gap widened from 0.09 (P2) to 0.74 (P3 strong at step 200). The strong-λ regime produces substantially larger train-val divergence. The model finds lower train loss under aux pressure but val doesn't follow at the same rate.

**This is the generalisation version of the "active LM degradation" reading I retracted yesterday.** The retracted reading said strong λs degrade LM as a persistent cost. The corrected reading at step 200 said no lasting effect after recovery, but the step 250 data reveals a more nuanced finding sitting between them: **the lasting effect is the gap, not the val.** Val recovers toward the P2 territory but the embedding configuration under aux pressure overfits training blocks in a way the unconstrained P2 configuration doesn't.

updated arg: 

*Strong auxiliary geometric pressure (λ_morph=50, λ_device=2) on 128K-vocab Llama at 3B parameters fails to move targeted geometric metrics, allows val to recover toward the P2 wall after initial disruption, AND produces a measurable widening of the train-val gap (0.09 → 0.74) that persists even after val recovery. The lasting cost of strong λs is generalisation, not LM fit.*

### Geometric null status: still locked

L_morph reads 0.0006 across all four batches at step 250. Identical to every previous reading from step 0 to step 250. λ=50 has produced zero observable movement across 250 steps. L_device 0.18-0.21 noise as always. The geometric null isn't softening with more training, it's more like a hard structural property of the loss landscape.
