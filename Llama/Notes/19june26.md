## 8B P1 complete (step 3000, val 11.485)

the final eval landed at val 11.485, train 92.66, indistinguishable from the plateau (11.48 / 11.48 / 11.484 / 11.485 across the last four evals). 

- **the Llama scale ladder is filled**: TinyLlama 1.1B, Llama 3.2-1B, Llama 3.2-3B, Llama 3.1-8B. four points across the family, all P1 done (three full pipelines + 8B P1).
- **the compositional-init experiment has its final verdict**: plateau at 11.485, never broke 11.40. clean negative result. the spherical-1.5x cohort reaches lower at every comparable scale. compositional init bought aggressive train descent (steepest in the lineup) that did not generalise to val. helped train, hurt generalisation. spherical 1.5x stays the cohort default, and 8B is the control that proves it.
- **the longest single P1 in the project**: 3000 steps across 29 sessions.

8B is the lineup's only model with compositional init at 1.0x radius and the longest P1 run in the Llama family at 29 sessions. the experiment: seed Wake tokens at semantically plausible starting points (from morpheme decomposition) instead of random spherical positions, so training doesn't waste steps finding home. the hypothesis was that this would buy faster, deeper convergence.

the result: **it did not pay off.** 8B plateaus at val 11.48, never breaking 11.40 and it produced the most extreme train-val divergence in the lineup where train descended ~20 points from step 1600 (114 to 92) while val drifted up +0.07 then flatlined. the compositional init bought aggressive *train* descent that did not translate to *val* improvement. the semantically-seeded init let the model exploit training-distribution structure that did not generalise. 

result: **compositional init does not improve val convergence; spherical 1.5x stays the cohort default.** this is the documented justification for the spherical-1.5x choice across the rest of the lineup, Phi included. 8B is the control that validates the default.
