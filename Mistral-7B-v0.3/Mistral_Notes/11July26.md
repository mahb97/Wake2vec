### the emoji finding and Joycean style

worth a reminder: 

1. the empirical claim: the P1 generation surfaced emoji at every temperature. the training corpus `FW_TEXT.txt` contains zero emoji. therefore every emoji in the output is, by elimination, coming from the frozen base pretraining distribution and nowhere else.
2. mechanism (architectural): P1 freezes the entire transformer and trains only the newly injected Wake embedding rows. the model's output distribution over the base vocabulary, emoji tokens included, is untouched at its pretrained values. when generation goes off-distribution (Wake prompt, high temperature, the babel), it samples from that frozen distribution, and Mistral's frozen distribution is internet-soaked, so the architecture explains why base priors surface, and the corpus proves they are base priors. 
3. comparative claim: the register that surfaces under deformation is a fingerprint of the base pretraining. Mistral (web-trained) surfaces emoji; the 8B (code-heavy pretraining) surfaces code-register tokens. same deformation pressure, two different substrates showing through. this is consistent with the P1 embedding-reorganisation results.
4. the interpretive claim: the Wake is itself a collision of registers and languages. a model pushed to imitate the Wake performs collision, but only of the registers it has, and Mistral's registers are internet-age (emoji, code), so its output is closer to Joyce's collision *method* run on a different substrate than to a copy of Joyce's specific collisions.
