## Methodological finding: accidental SGDR via STEP_OFFSET resume

train spikes happen at every session resume. step 1700 (232), step 1850 (220), step 2200 (211), step 2400 (200+). each spike sits within the first few logging intervals after a resume. Because Qwen uses manual resume (not Trainer-native, unlike Llama), every session calls `trainer.train()` fresh and so the Trainer's internal step counter restarts at 0 and the LR scheduler restarts with it. with cosine schedule, step 0 sits near peak LR. 

so at every session:

1. wake overlay weights load from sentry (correct)
2. LR scheduler reinitialises at near-peak LR (much higher than schedule should be at global step N)
3. first batches get hit with the elevated LR
4. train loss spikes from larger gradient updates
5. schedule decays normally through the session

what happens *after* each train spike:

| Step | Train spike | Val response |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 1700 | 232.25 | 16.01 to 15.89 (first 16.0 break) |
| 1850 | 220.09 | 15.79 to 15.72 |
| 2200 | 211.59 | 15.53 to 15.45 |
| 2400 | 202.52 | 15.55 to 15.42 |

the val drops *follow* the train spikes, thus the LR resets are kicking embeddings out of plateaus and into new descents.

this is accidentally a form of **SGDR**, see [Stochastic Gradient Descent with Warm Restarts, Loshchilov and Hutter 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1608.03983). periodic LR resets break local minima and let the model escape suboptimal regions of the loss landscape, and i've only just discovered that the Qween has been doing accidental SGDR for ~33 sessions.

**implication:** Qwen is the only model in the lineup that never plateaued in P1 but instead keeps descending. the Llamas use Trainer-native resume which restores LR scheduler state and so behaves as continuous cosine decay with no warm restarts. they plateau early because they have to while Qwen doesn't plateau because the manual-resume pattern accidentally gave it implicit warm restarts.

**paper note:** this is a methodological finding the project gets for free because of how the WakeOverlay plus STEP_OFFSET resume pattern was designed, as with many other things in Wake2vec, this script wasn't intended as a warm-restart experiment, but it has became one, and the result is the only model with a monotonic descent through P1's full 3000 steps.
