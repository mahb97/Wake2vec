## Qwen 2.5-14B P2 session 1 (launching from P1 best-val, step 2700)

P2 is live so the Qween moves from completed P1 canonical into LoRA behavioural adaptation. After the canonical landed on the 9th, after the generation battery falsified the simple smaller-model paradox on the 11th, today she starts her second phase.

### Architecture transition: WakeOverlay to standard LoRA

P1 used WakeOverlay (base frozen, separate Wake-row matrix trained, no LoRA). P2 reverts to the project-standard P2 protocol, the same one Llama 3B P2 ran:

1. Freezes ALL parameters including embeddings (P2 is behavioural adaptation, not embedding adaptation)
3. Applies LoRA (r=8, α=16) to q/k/v/gate/up/down projections
3. Trains ONLY the LoRA adapters

### P1 source: step 2700

the P2 source is the **P1 best-val sentry (step 2700, val 15.05, the 6th SGDR low)**, not the canonical endpoint (step 3000, val 15.09) which matches the TinyLlama, 1B and 3B P2 convention, which launched from P1 best-val rather than the P1 endpoint.

## Notes

### What the first eval will tell us

Llama 3B P2 hit the wall at step 100: val dropped from P1 best 6.68 to 5.33 (a -1.35 jump), then never moved across six consecutive evals. The question for Qwen P2:

- **If Qwen P2 walls early** (val drops ~1-2 from P1 best 15.05 then plateaus): the LoRA ceiling is universal across the 128K-vocab-and-above family AND extends to the WakeOverlay architecture. Cross-architecture finding stronger than 3B alone establishes.
- **If Qwen P2 keeps descending** past the first few evals: scale + WakeOverlay breaks the LoRA ceiling that 3B couldn't. 

with EVAL_STEPS=50, the wall (if it comes) gets documented across ~250 steps to declare termination rather than the ~500 a 100-step cadence would need. faster termination if Qwen walls like 3B did.

## Addendum: first two evals. Qwen P2 does NOT wall but descends hard.

| Step | Train | Val | vs P1 best (15.05) |
|------|-------|-----|--------------------|
| 50 | 167.44 | 15.69 | +0.64 (LoRA init perturbation) |
| 100 | 175.05 | **13.43** | **-1.62** |

**This is the opposite of the 3B P2 wall.** 

- Step 50: val 15.69, slightly ABOVE P1 best. This is the LoRA-initialisation perturbation so the freshly-added adapters start at random and briefly disrupt the inherited configuration before they learn anything useful. 3B P2 showed the same brief initial bump.
- Step 100: val **13.43**, a 2.26-point drop from step 50 and 1.62 below the P1 best-val of 15.05. **Below 14.**

Compare the two architectures at the equivalent point:

| | P1 best val | P2 step 100 val | Δ | Behaviour |
|---|-------------|------------------|---|-----------|
| Llama 3.2-3B | 6.68 | 5.33 | -1.35 then WALL | LoRA dropped val once, then 6 identical evals |
| **Qwen 2.5-14B** | **15.05** | **13.43** | **-1.62 and still descending** | **no wall yet; steepest single-eval drop of the run** |

3B P2's -1.35 drop at step 100 was the LoRA capacity ceiling being reached in one shot, after which nothing moved for 500 steps. Qwen P2's -1.62 drop at step 100 looks different: the train loss went UP (167 → 175) while val went DOWN (15.69 → 13.43), which is the signature of LoRA actively learning generalisable structure rather than hitting a ceiling. If this were a wall, train and val would both flatten. Instead val is dropping faster than train, which means the adapters are finding real behavioural adaptation that transfers to held-out blocks.

**What this means for the cross-architecture LoRA-ceiling finding:**

The 3B P2 wall at val 5.33 was framed as possibly universal: "128K-vocab Llama at 3B cannot break val 5.33 via LoRA." Qwen P2's early descent is the first evidence that **the wall is NOT universal across the family at higher scale**. The provisional reading: scale (14B vs 3B) and/or the WakeOverlay-then-LoRA architecture gives Qwen enough representational headroom that LoRA finds real descent where 3B's LoRA hit a ceiling immediately.

This interacts with the refined smaller-model paradox from yesterday. The Qween's story is now: brute-force-efficient P1 (scale + 14 weeks + 39 SGDR cycles) + dense polyglot Wake generation + a P2 LoRA phase that actually descends instead of walling. The 14B scale keeps paying off at every phase. Whether it walls eventually (at val 12? 11?) or keeps descending toward the Llama 3B territory is the question the next evals answer.
