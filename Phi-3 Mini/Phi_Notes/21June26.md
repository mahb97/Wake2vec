## footnote on step 300 the first textbook-resists signal

step 300 landed at train 11.64, val 12.233. compared to step 100 (train 12.40, val 12.25): **train descended 0.76, val moved 0.017.** the val is essentially flat, barely above its 11.25 random baseline, while train pulls away from it. this is the "val barely moves (~12)" branch of the textbook-vs-internet hypothesis, the first hint that textbook priors resist Wake.

### resists generalisation, not memorisation

train is descending, so the model is memorising Wake training blocks fine. what it is not doing is generalising them. the clean-reasoning representation absorbs the Wake tokens locally (train down) but will not reorganise into a generalisable Wake subspace (val flat). **textbook training does not block Wake memorisation; it resists Wake generalisation.** the orderly English representation is too rigid to deform into a held-out-generalising Joyce subspace, even while it overfits the training blocks.
