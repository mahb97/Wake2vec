step 400 landed at train 11.475, val 12.247. the full trajectory: val 12.25 (100), 12.233 (300), 12.247 (400). the val never meaningfully descended instead it dipped 0.017 to a shallow minimum at step 300 (12.233) and is now rising (12.247) while train descended ~0.93 (12.40 to 11.475). the train-val gap is widening and the val U-curve already turned at a minimum barely below where it started.

Seems like **Textbook training resists Wake generalisation.** Phi memorises Wake training blocks (train descends steadily) but never forms a generalisable Wake subspace (val pinned ~1 nat above the 11.25 random baseline, never leaving the random neighbourhood). The clean-reasoning representation is rigid enough that, even at 58% Wake share and spherical 1.5x init, it absorbs Wake tokens locally without reorganising into a representation that transfers to held-out Wake.

# data note

textbook data is filtered for clean, orderly, high-signal English which produces a rigid, well-structured English representation, optimised for the reasoning the data was curated for, butFinnegans Wake is the corpus at maximum distance from that filter. the rigid representation can memorise individual Wake tokens (local) but cannot deform into a generalisable Wake subspace (global), because deforming would mean unlearning the orderliness the textbook training installed. internet-trained models (Mistral, TinyLlama) have messier, more deformable representations that reorganise readily toward Joyce. So **knowing "textbook" English makes a model worse at learning to break it**, which is the exact branch the launch-day hypothesis flagged as the textbook-resists outcome.

## Notes

### claim: resists generalisation rather than memorisation
 
textbook training resists Wake *generalisation* (val flat near baseline), it does not block Wake *memorisation* (Phi's train descends, 12.40 to 11.64). the clean-reasoning representation absorbs Wake tokens locally but will not reorganise into a generalisable Wake subspace. 

### μp → UP

Phi's μ-units are being memorised on the training data (train descends) but not generalising (val flat). the micro-units are absorbed locally without composing into a generalisable subspace. compared to Mistral, whose μ-reorganization was the deepest in the lineup. the textbook prior resists the reorganization Mistral did.
